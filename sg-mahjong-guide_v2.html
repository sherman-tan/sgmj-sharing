What is Mahjong?

The basics before you even touch a tile

Mahjong (麻將) is a tile-based game for 4 players. Think of it like a super fun version of Rummy — you draw tiles, discard tiles, and try to be the first to assemble a complete winning hand.

Singapore Mahjong has its own unique rules — it's faster, simpler, and cashier-friendly compared to Hong Kong or Chinese styles. It's what your aunties play at CNY. 🧧

The Goal

  • Collect 14 tiles that form a winning hand (胡牌 — "Hu Pai")
  • Be the first player to win. Declare "HU!" (胡) when you win
  • A winning hand = 4 sets + 1 pair (with some bonus patterns)
🃏 Quick Facts ✅ 4 players  |  ✅ 144 tiles  |  ✅ Takes ~1–2 hours per session
✅ Each round: East player goes first  |  ✅ Points = cash (usually $0.10–$0.50 per point)

三

Setting Up the Game

Building the wall and dealing tiles

Before the fun begins, everyone helps set up the "wall" and deal tiles. And before ANY of that — you need to know which direction things go!

🔄 Clockwise vs Anti-Clockwise — Know This First!

In Mahjong, the table has two different directions happening at once. This confuses beginners the most, so let's nail it right here.

🔁 ANTI-CLOCKWISE (逆時針)
— Turn Order & Dealing Play moves anti-clockwise around the table:

East → North → West → South → East…

Think of it as: the person to your RIGHT goes after you. Dealing also goes anti-clockwise.
🔃 CLOCKWISE (順時針)
— Wind Rotation After each round, the East (dealer) seat rotates clockwise:

East seat → passes to the player on East's left

i.e. the seat rotates clockwise even though play goes anti-clockwise.

Step 1: Shuffle & Build the Wall

  • Shuffle ALL 144 tiles face-down on the table
  • Each player builds the wall in front of them — 2 tiles high, with lengths alternating: East & West build 19 wide, North & South build 18 wide
  • Total: 19 + 18 + 19 + 18 = 74 stacks × 2 high = 148 tile positions (4 spare — this is correct)
  • Push all 4 rows together to form a square "wall" in the center

⚠️ Why 148 slots for 144 tiles? The 4 extra slots are intentional — they account for the corner overlaps where walls meet. Some tables have East & West do 19, and North & South do 18. The important thing: your aunties will tell you exactly how they do it — just follow the host's setup! 😄

Step 2: Determine East (Dealer)

  • Roll the dice to determine who is East (the dealer) for the first round
  • East goes first and deals tiles
  • After East loses a round, the East seat passes clockwise (to the player on East's left)
  • The wind round advances when all 4 players have been East once

Step 3: Deal the Tiles (Anti-Clockwise)

  • Roll dice again to determine where to break the wall (your starting draw point)
  • Deal tiles in groups of 4, going anti-clockwise: East gets 4 → North gets 4 → West gets 4 → South gets 4
  • Repeat 3 more times (so everyone has 12 tiles)
  • East draws 2 more tiles (getting 14 total); everyone else draws 1 more tile (getting 13 total)
💡 Why does East get 14? East starts with 14 tiles because they go first. Everyone else has 13. On your turn, you draw to get 14, then discard to return to 13. Simple!

Step 4: Reveal Flowers

  • After dealing, each player checks for flower/season tiles
  • Place any flowers face-up in front of you, then draw replacement tile(s) from the back of the wall
  • Repeat until you have no more flowers in your hand
  • East begins play once all flowers are declared
🎲 The Dice Rule (Breaking the Wall) After determining East, roll the dice again. Count that number of stacks from the right end of East's wall — that's your break point. Drawing begins from the LEFT side of that break, going anti-clockwise. The right side of the break becomes the "kong end" (back of wall) for replacement draws. Don't overthink this as a beginner — just let experienced players guide you the first time!

But Wait — You Can Steal Discards!

When someone discards a tile, the OTHER players can grab it. Here's the priority order:

🥇 Hu (胡)
Win — highest priority
Any player can declare Hu if the discarded tile completes their winning hand. Always beats everything.
🥈 Kong (槓)
4 of a kind
Any player who has 3 tiles matching the discard can declare Kong. Draw a replacement from the back wall.
🥉 Pong (碰)
3 of a kind
Any player who has 2 matching tiles can Pong any discard. Reveal the set, then discard a tile.
Chow (吃)
Run of 3
Only the next player (the one whose turn it would be) can Chow. Forms a sequence in the same suit.
📌 Key Rule: Chow is Lower Priority If two players want a tile — one for Pong and one for Chow — Pong wins. Hu always wins over everything. If nobody claims a discard, the next player simply draws from the wall.
🇸🇬 Singapore Rule: Chow Restriction + Direction Because play moves anti-clockwise, the player who acts just before you sits to your right. You can ONLY Chow from the player on your right (i.e. the one who just discarded). You cannot Chow from across the table or from your left.

Memory aid: "Chow only from the RIGHT — because they go before you, and play goes anti-clockwise."
🔄 Anti-Clockwise Play — Visualised Sitting as South: your right = East (they go before you), your left = West (they go after you).
Turn order: East → North → West → South → East…
So when East discards, North can Chow it. When North discards, West can Chow it. When West discards, South (you!) can Chow it.

Declaring a Meld (Open Hand)

  • When you Pong, Chow, or Kong, you reveal the meld face-up in front of you
  • Your hand is now "open" — this limits some winning patterns you can claim
  • After revealing, discard one tile to bring your hand back to the right count

Quick Check 🤔

Player South discards a tile. Player West has 2 of the same tile and wants to Pong. Player East wants to Chow (it completes a sequence). Who gets the tile?

Scoring & Payment

How points translate to who pays whom

Singapore Mahjong scoring uses "tai" (台) as the unit. Most games have a minimum of 3 tai to win. Each tai has a dollar value agreed before the game.

💰 Example Game Value If 1 tai = $0.50, then a 3-tai win = $1.50 per payer. The loser (person who discarded the winning tile) pays you 3× that. Others may pay reduced amounts.

Who Pays?

  • Win on discard: Only the player who discarded the winning tile pays you
  • Self-draw (自摸): ALL 3 other players pay you — this is why it's more valuable!
  • When East wins or East pays, the amount is often doubled (East premium)

Key Rules & Etiquette

Don't be the blur one at the table

Knowing the rules is one thing; knowing table etiquette is another. These will save you from awkward moments!

Important Rules

  • Chuck (放炮 Fàng Pào): If you discard the tile that lets someone win, you've "chucked" — you pay the full amount alone
  • False Hu penalty: Declaring Hu when you don't actually have a valid winning hand is a penalty — you pay everyone a fixed amount
  • Dead hand: If you've exposed tiles incorrectly, your hand may be "dead" — you can still play but cannot win the round
  • Kong replacement: After declaring a Kong, always draw from the BACK of the wall, not the front
  • Robbing a Kong: If someone upgrades a Pong to a Kong using a tile you need to Hu, you CAN steal it for your win!

🤝 Table Etiquette

  • Discard tiles face-up and clearly so everyone can see
  • Announce "Pong!", "Chow!", "Kong!", or "Hu!" loudly and clearly before picking up the tile
  • Don't touch the discarded tile before announcing your claim
  • Arrange your open melds neatly in front of you for all to see
  • Keep your hand tiles hidden — don't flash them to other players
  • Don't take too long — "playing slowly" (慢慢打) is frowned upon
  • If you're almost winning, don't hint or celebrate early — bad luck! 🙅

🇸🇬 Singapore-Specific Rules (vs HK/Chinese)

Min Tai Required
Usually 3 tai minimum to declare Hu. HK style uses "fan" instead.
Chow Restriction
Only the player whose turn it would be can Chow (left of discarder).
Flowers
Flowers give bonus tai. Always draw replacement from back wall.
East Premium
When East wins or East pays, amounts are typically doubled.
No Jokers
Singapore Mahjong does not use Joker tiles (unlike some variations).

Only the chucker pays on a discard win. All 3 pay on self-draw.

💡 Beginner Tips

  • Stay flexible: Don't commit to one winning pattern too early. Keep your options open
  • Track discards: Watch what others throw — if you need a tile 3 people have already discarded, you'll never get it
  • Defend: If someone looks close to winning, avoid discarding tiles that complete triplets or pairs
  • Value Pongs: Honour tiles (winds, dragons) are worth more — prioritise collecting matching ones
  • Don't be shy: Shout "Pong!" or "Hu!" clearly. Better to be heard than to miss your claim!
  • Ask questions: Your tablemates WANT you to learn — asking is totally fine for first-timers

Common House Rules

Every family does it a little differently — here's what to ask before you sit down

Singapore Mahjong has no single "official" rulebook — every household, every group of aunties, every CNY table has its own tweaks. Knowing the common variants will save you from arguments (and accidental debt).

✅ Pro Tip: Ask These 5 Questions Before Every Game 1. What's the minimum tai to win?  |  2. What's the tai value ($)?
3. Are Animals included?  |  4. Does East double?  |  5. Any special winning hands?

House RuleHow It WorksCommon At

3 Tai Min Your hand must total ≥3 tai before you can declare Hu. Forces players to build stronger hands. Stricter family tables, older generation
No Min (0 Tai) Some ultra-casual games allow any valid 14-tile hand to win, even 0 tai. Rare. Very casual / teaching games

💰 Shooter & Payment Variants

House RuleHow It Works
Shooter pays all (Standard) On a discard win (放炮), ONLY the person who chucked pays. Others pay nothing.
Shooter pays double Shooter pays 2× normal. Others pay 1× each. Punishes careless discards harder.
Split payment Shooter pays half; the other two non-winners split the other half. Softens the chuck penalty.
Everyone pays on discard Even on a chuck, all 3 non-winners pay (like self-draw). Rare but exists.
East doubles (莊家加倍) When East wins or East is the shooter, all payments are ×2. Very common SG rule.

🀄 Winning Hand Variants

House RuleEffect
Earthly Hand (地胡) South/West/North wins on their FIRST draw from the wall (before discarding). Massive payout like Heavenly Hand.
Thirteen Orphans (十三么) Special hand: one each of 1/9 Bamboo, 1/9 Circles, 1/9 Characters, all 4 Winds, all 3 Dragons + 1 duplicate. If your table allows it, massive payout — often "pay-all" regardless of who wins.
All Kong hand (十八羅漢) Four Kongs + 1 pair. Extremely rare. Most tables pay all three opponents a large fixed amount.
Seven Pairs (七對) Seven distinct pairs instead of 4 sets + 1 pair. Not all tables allow this — ask first!
Robbing the Kong (搶槓) If someone upgrades a Pong to a Kong with your winning tile, you can steal it for Hu. Most tables allow this.
Last tile win (海底捞月) Winning on the very last tile drawn from the wall. +1 tai bonus at most tables.
Win on replacement (嶺上開花) Winning on the tile drawn after a Kong. +1 or +2 tai bonus.

No flowers Some faster-paced games remove flowers entirely. Speeds up the game significantly.

🔄 Scoring & Multiplier Variants

House RuleEffect
Cap at X tai Some tables cap maximum payout at e.g. 10 tai regardless of hand. Prevents runaway losses.
Double on special hands Pure suit, all pongs etc. double the total payment instead of adding flat tai.
Consecutive East bonus If East wins multiple rounds in a row, bonus multiplier stacks (×2, ×3…). Very punishing!
Draw = no payment If the wall is exhausted with no winner (荒牌 Huāng Pái), nobody pays. Game just redeals.
False Hu penalty If you declare Hu incorrectly, you pay each other player a fixed amount (usually 3 tai equivalent). Your hand is dead for the round.
⚠️ The Golden Rule When in doubt about any rule — ask before the game starts, not after you win. Arguing about rules mid-game or after payment is the fastest way to ruin the mood. Most experienced players are happy to explain their house rules upfront!
